London, England

April 2023

In the spring of 2023, just before my high school graduation, I traveled to London to visit my uncle and attend a Harry Styles concert—a gift I had been eagerly anticipating. It was my first time going to a concert alone, and the thought of being in a different country made me nervous. At first, everything felt unfamiliar—the way people moved with a certain poise felt distinct, and hearing all the different accents gave the city a dreamlike, almost storybook quality. But as I settled in, I began to embrace the excitement of exploring a new city on my own. I spent an entire week in London, and in addition to the concert, I made it my mission to see all the famous tourist attractions.

The concert itself was an unforgettable experience. I had an incredible view from the pit, and the energy in the arena was electric. The entire crowd sang along to every song, completely lost in the music, and for a moment, I forgot I was alone in a foreign country. Being surrounded by so many people who shared the same love for Harry Styles made me feel connected in a way I hadn’t expected. The lights, the music, and the overwhelming joy in the room created a moment that felt almost magical—one I knew I’d never forget.

Beyond the concert, I fell in love with London. I visited iconic landmarks like Buckingham Palace, the Tower of London, and Big Ben, each more breathtaking in person than in pictures. The city's architecture was stunning, with its grand historic buildings and charming streets. The air felt fresher, and despite what many say about British food, I actually enjoyed it. One of the highlights of my trip was trying Nando’s for the first time—it was easily one of the best meals I’ve ever had, and to this day, I still crave it. My trip to London wasn’t just about the concert; it was an experience that gave me confidence, new perspectives, and memories I’ll cherish forever.
