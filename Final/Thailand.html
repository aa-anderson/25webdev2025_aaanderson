Bangkok, Thailand

August 2018

In August 2018, I celebrated my birthday with a week-long trip to Bangkok, Thailand—an experience that was both thrilling and unforgettable. From the moment I stepped off the plane, the city welcomed me with vibrant colors, fragrant street food, and a constant hum of energy. My days were filled with exploration: visiting majestic temples like Wat Arun and Wat Pho, navigating the city’s famous floating markets, and riding tuk-tuks through winding alleys that always led somewhere unexpected. Everything felt new and alive, and I loved the way Bangkok balanced deep tradition with modern buzz.

The food was easily one of the most memorable parts of the trip. Every meal felt like a celebration—spicy curries, papaya salad, coconut soups, and, of course, mango sticky rice that I still dream about. Whether I was eating from a tiny roadside stand or a cozy restaurant tucked away on a quiet street, the flavors were bold, comforting, and absolutely unforgettable. I even took a cooking class and learned to make pad Thai from scratch—something I proudly attempted again when I got home.

Celebrating my birthday in such a vibrant and welcoming place felt incredibly special. I didn’t just explore a new country—I also connected with locals, made new friends, and created memories that made that birthday feel like a turning point. It reminded me how valuable travel can be in shaping who we are and how we see the world. Bangkok didn’t just give me a fun week—it gave me a deeper sense of appreciation for culture, spontaneity, and celebration.
